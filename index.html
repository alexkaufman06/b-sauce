1953 Iranian Coup d'etat

Waltz's 3 Level Analysis

This paper analyzes the 1953 Iranian Coup d' etat under the lens of the Waltz 3 Level Analysis of International Relations. In it I argue that Mossadegh, Churchill and Eisenhower, Fazalollah Zahedi and British Society are first image sources. While the CIA, and nationalistic tendencies are the second category. Lastly the third level includes Britian-U.S. relations, Cold War relations and control of Iranian oil.

International Political Economy

Comments on Multinational Trade

This paper examines the question of Are Multinational Corporations a force for development of mechanisms of impoverishment. I argue that Multinational Corporations are mechanisms of impoverishment for the developing nations they do business with. They seek to minimize restrictions while exploiting cheap labor and natural resources. Lastly they reinforce developmental dependency on the actions of the multinational capital.

Less Developed Countries Dilemma

Here I examine the paradox that Less Developed Countries face while trying to achieve a higher state of economic well- being. They need to be integrated into the world economy in order to develop, however they are faced with strict monetary regulations from international bodies and political pressures which curtail their development. I argue that ultimately each nation needs to structure a unique specific plan for themselves by examining what past policies have been successful.

Comparing Marx vs. Mill Ideology

In this essay I examine the differences between Mill's idea of "custom" expressed in his book On Liberty and Marx's "ideology" expressed in his work the The German Ideology. I focus on how Marx critiques ideological thought and would argue that Mill's idea of "custom" and the individual are ideological constructions. I also analyze their thoughts in connection to the mode of production.

A comparison of two women during the Civil War

This is a research paper I wrote where I examined the role Southern and Northern women played in the Civil War. I did a case study on the lives of two elite women: northerner Septima Collins and southerner Mary Chestnut. I analyzed their diaries and used this sources to compare their experiences during the war. Although these women do not represent all American women during the Civil War, they may offer insight into how women's lives as a whole were changed due to the war, with southern women suffering a great deal more than northern women.

WeB DuBois Commentary

Here I analyze how W.E.B DuBois's book, The World and Africa, fits into the greater Cold War narrative. In this book, Dubois illustrates how fear about the spread of communism and issues of race relations have led the Western world to completely oppress many African nations and that this should not be allowed to continue. Although the book did not have great significance at the time it was published, Du Bois is still a significant Cold War figure because he stands for everything the Cold War is not. He takes a stance for African rights and wants to stop the exploitation of developing nations by Imperial powers.
